Use this service to report the result from a free NHS rapid lateral flow test:

  • every time you do a test, no matter what it is - positive, negative or void
  • within 24 hours of getting the result
{{ govukWarningText({ text: "You cannot use this service to report results from a test kit you’ve paid for.", iconFallbackText: "Warning" }) }} {% set detailsContent %}

You must report a positive test result within 5 days of developing symptoms.

If you test negative but still have symptoms, you should take another test for the next 2 days.

If you report a positive result, you should be contacted within 24 hours by an NHS Covid-19 specialist to assess if you are eligible for anti-viral medication. Make sure you provide your NHS number, postcode and phone number so you can be contacted.

If you are not contacted within 24 hours, you should call your GP, 111, or your specialist clinician who can make an urgent referral.

Before you start

To use this service, you need:

  • the QR code or ID number printed on the free NHS test strip
  • a mobile phone number so we can text you to confirm we’ve got your result

Check the expiry date on the box the test came in. If your test is out of date:

  • do not report the result
  • take a new test from a box that is still in date
  • dispose of any tests that are out of date - recycle what you can and put the rest in your general rubbish bin

Get help to report your free NHS test

If you cannot use the online service, call 119 (free from mobiles and landlines).

Lines are open:

  • Monday to Friday (including bank holidays), 8am to 6pm
  • Saturday and Sunday, 9am to 1pm

119 provides support in 200 languages.

SignVideo is a free online British Sign Language (BSL) interpreter service for 119.

You can use the free Be My Eyes app to get help from trained NHS Test and Trace staff. Download the app, go to 'Specialized Help' and select 'NHS Test & Trace' in the 'Personal Health' category.

If you have a problem with your test kit

If anything in your free NHS COVID-19 test kit was missing or damaged, or you were harmed using the test kit, report the problem online or call 119.

Do not use the test kit if any part of it is missing or damaged, or if the test kit is out of date.
